AS 5369:2023

Reprocessing of Reusable Medical Devices (RMDs)

Overview of AS 5369:2023

AS 5369:2023 is the primary Australian Standard governing the cleaning, disinfection, and sterilisation of Reusable Medical Devices (RMDs). Effective from December 2023, it supersedes both AS/NZS 4815 and AS/NZS 4187, unifying hospital and office-based practices under one rigorous framework.

A key shift in AS 5369:2023 is the mandatory integration of a documented Quality Management System (QMS). For the Dental Assistant, adherence to this standard means that all processes must not only be performed correctly but must be strictly documented, validated, and aligned with the manufacturer's Instructions for Use (IFU) to mitigate healthcare-associated infections (HAIs).

Validated Cleaning Processes

Under AS 5369:2023, sterilisation cannot effectively occur without meticulous, validated cleaning. Bioburden protects microorganisms from sterilising agents.

  • Manufacturer IFUs: The standard mandates strict adherence to the RMD manufacturer's Instructions for Use (IFU) for all cleaning processes.
  • Automated Cleaning: There is a strong directive towards automated cleaning (e.g., Thermal Disinfectors) over manual cleaning to ensure reproducible, validated outcomes.
  • Visual Inspection: Prior to packaging, all RMDs must undergo rigorous visual inspection, using magnification and adequate lighting as specified by the standard, to verify the removal of all soil.

Segregated Workflow and Facility Design

AS 5369:2023 places heavy emphasis on the physical environment to prevent cross-contamination.

  • Designated Areas: Reprocessing facilities must have clear, physical separation between "dirty" (decontamination) and "clean" (packaging/sterilisation) zones.
  • Unidirectional Flow: RMDs and personnel must follow a strict one-way workflow from point-of-use → cleaning → packaging → sterilisation → storage. Backtracking is strictly prohibited to maintain the integrity of clean zones.

Routine Monitoring and Release

AS 5369:2023 moves away from assuming a cycle worked just because it finished, requiring documented evidence for the release of RMDs.

  • Parametric Release: The DA must verify that the physical parameters (time, temperature, pressure) recorded on the steriliser's logger match the validated Performance Qualification (PQ) profile for that specific load type.
  • Chemical Indicators (CIs): Class II indicators are required to have visibly passed the heat process - these are displayed on the outside of every pack or pouch or through the sterilising tape used to seal a pack.

Traceability and Quality Management

As part of the QMS required by AS 5369:2023, an unbroken audit trail is mandatory.

  • Batch Control Identification (BCI): Every processed RMD must be traceable to the patient, the specific steriliser cycle, and the operator who released the load.
  • Recall Protocols: Records must be maintained to enable the immediate identification and recall of any non-conforming RMDs if a sterilisation failure is detected post-release.

AS 5369:2023 mandates that gross soil removal begins immediately at the point of use. RMDs must be kept moist during transport to the decontamination area to prevent biofilm formation.

Water Quality Standards

The standard specifies precise limits for microbial and chemical water quality. Final rinse water and steam generation water must be regularly tested to prevent RMD damage and ensure process efficacy.

Equipment Validation

(Tap to reveal)

Installation Qualification (IQ), Operational Qualification (OQ), and annual Performance Qualification (PQ) are strictly required for all thermal disinfectors and sterilisers.

Event-Related Sterility

(Tap to reveal)

Storage requirements focus on event-related sterility. RMDs must be stored in dedicated, access-controlled environments that protect against moisture, dust, and temperature fluctuations.

💡
Standard Alignment: Unlike older guidelines, AS 5369:2023 is absolute in its requirement for documented proof. For the Dental Assistant, "If it isn't documented according to the facility's QMS, it didn't happen."
